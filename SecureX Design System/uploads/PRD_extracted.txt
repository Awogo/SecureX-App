1. Product Overview

SecureX is an integrated FinTech prototype designed to enable safe, trusted, and transparent transactions between African SMEs and their customers. The platform combines smart escrow payment protection, trust scoring, and automated transaction records to address the dual challenge of transaction distrust and poor business management in Africa’s informal and semi-formal economy.

SecureX is built as a Progressive Web Application (PWA) and demonstrates how secure transaction infrastructure can support SME growth, financial inclusion, and cross-border trade within Africa.

This project delivers a functional prototype for demonstration and evaluation purposes and does not process real financial transactions.

2. Problem Statement

Currently, African SME owners, traders, and their customers struggle with unsafe and non-transparent buying and selling transactions, coupled with inefficient financial record-keeping and limited business intelligence capabilities. This dual challenge leads to lost revenue, customer distrust, fraud incidents, poor cash flow management, and restricted access to growth opportunities.

African SMEs face two critical and interrelated challenges:

Lack of transaction trust between buyers and sellers, especially during first-time, high-value, or cross-border transactions. Buyers fear fraud and non-delivery, while sellers fear non-payment and disputes.

Inefficient financial record-keeping, with over 70% of SMEs relying on paper or memory-based tracking, leading to poor financial visibility and limited access to credit.

These challenges result in:

Lost sales and abandoned transactions.

Fraud incidents and disputes.

Poor cash flow management.

Inability to access loans or formal financial services.

This problem occurs daily across physical markets, online channels, and informal trade networks throughout Africa.

FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS

Functional requirements describe what SecureX must do.

They define the system’s behavior, features, and business logic.

1.1 User Registration &amp; Account Management:

FR-01: The system shall allow users to register as either Buyer or Seller using a phone number, email address and password.(Registration)

FR-02: The system shall provide mechanism to reset password and recover accounts via email.(Recovery)

FR-03: The system shall verify user identity through OTP using phone number /email verification during registration.(Verification)

FR-04: The system shall support tiered KYC verification (Phone Verification → Government ID Upload → Business Registration).

FR-05: The system shall assign role-based permissions (Buyer, Seller, Admin).

FR-06: The system shall allow users to securely log in and log out. (Account Control)

FR-07: The system shall allow users to update their profile information.(Account Control)

FR-08: The system shall allow users to deactivate or delete their accounts.(Account Control)

1.2 SafePay Link &amp; Transaction Initiation:

FR-08: The system shall allow sellers to generate a unique SafePay payment link containing:

Item description

Price

Currency

Seller ID

Transaction reference number

FR-08: The system shall allow buyers to access a secure payment page via the SafePay link.

FR-09: The system shall validate payment amount before processing.

1.3 Escrow &amp; Payment Processing:

FR-10: The system shall lock funds in a secure escrow account upon successful buyer payment (card, bank transfer, or mobile money).

FR-11: The system shall generate a unique transaction ID for every escrowed payment.

FR-12: The system shall notify both buyer and seller via OTP,push notification and SMS when funds are successfully secured in escrow.

FR-13: The system shall record all escrow transactions in a digital ledger.

1.4 Proof of Delivery &amp; Fund Release:

FR-14: The system shall generate a unique 6-digit OTP upon payment confirmation.

FR-15: The system shall transmit the OTP securely to the buyer’s registered device.

FR-16: The system shall allow the seller to enter the OTP in their interface to confirm delivery.

FR-17: The system shall release escrowed funds to the seller only after successful OTP validation.

FR-18: The system shall implement a 48-hour inspection window after delivery confirmation.

FR-19: The system shall automatically release funds if no dispute is raised within 48 hours.

1.5 Dispute &amp; Risk Management:

FR-20: The system shall allow buyers to raise disputes within the inspection window.

FR-21: The system shall temporarily freeze funds during an active dispute.

FR-22: The system shall log dispute history in the transaction ledger.

1.6 TrustScore System and Verification:

FR-23: The system shall calculate a dynamic TrustScore(0–100) for each user.

FR-24: The TrustScore™ shall be calculated based on defined signals such as:

Transaction completion rate.

Dispute rate.

KYC level.

Delivery confirmation speed.

FR-25: The system shall update the TrustScore in real-time after transaction completion or dispute.

FR-26: The system shall display TrustScore and verification badges and rating on public seller profiles.

FR-27: The system shall flag accounts exhibiting suspicious behavior patterns.

1.7 Business Intelligence &amp; Analytics:

FR-28: The system shall automatically record all completed transactions in a digital ledger.

FR-29: The system shall generate real-time dashboards showing:

Daily revenue.

Weekly revenue.

Monthly revenue.

Transaction volume.

FR-30: The system shall allow sellers to export a Digital Business Passport (PDF) containing verified transaction history.

FR-31: The system shall provide predictive inventory insights based on historical sales data (conceptual AI model).

1.8 Accessibility &amp; Inclusion:

FR-32: The system shall support a Progressive Web App (PWA) interface optimized for mobile devices.

FR-33: The system shall provide a USSD interface for delivery confirmation and wallet balance checks (simulation allowed).

FR-34: The system shall support multi-currency transactions and lock exchange rates at escrow initiation.

FR-35: The system shall simulate integration with government ID verification APIs (e.g., NIN, IPRS).

NON-FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS

Non-functional requirements define how well SecureX performs its functions.

They define performance, reliability, security, scalability, and quality standards.

2.1 Performance:

NFR-01: Escrow confirmation notifications shall be delivered within 2 seconds under normal network conditions.

NFR-02: OTP generation and validation shall occur within 2 seconds under normal network conditions.

The system shall comfortably handle at least 1,500 simultaneous users (Customers and Vendors/Sellers) without significant performance degradation.

NFR-03: The system shall load core screens within 2 seconds under normal network conditions.

2.2 Availability:

NFR-04: The system shall maintain an uptime of 99.9% (less than 8.7 hours of unscheduled downtime per year)

NFR-05: The system shall log all transaction errors, system failures, and security attempts for quick diagnosis and maintenance.

2.3 Security:

NFR-06: All personal and financial data (passwords, payment details, contact info etc.) shall be secured using end-to-end encryption (e.g., TLS/SSL, HTTPS/TLS)protocols.

NFR-07: Sensitive data shall be encrypted at rest using AES-256 encryption and shall comply with all local and international data privacy laws when collecting, storing, and processing user data.

NFR-08: The system shall follow and be compliant with relevant payment industry standards PCI-DSS best practices for payment processing to ensure the security of customer payments.

NFR-09: OTP codes shall expire 3minutes. 

NFR-10: The system shall implement role-based access control.

2.4 Scalability:

NFR-11: The architecture shall conceptually support up to 70,000 concurrent transactions.

NFR-12: The system shall be horizontally scalable.

2.5 Usability:

NFR-13: The system shall follow mobile-first UX design principles.

NFR-14: The UI shall use clear language and high-contrast design for low-literacy users.

NFR-15: The system shall minimize required user steps for completing transactions.

2.6 Reliability &amp; Data Integrity:

NFR-16: The system shall ensure zero data loss during transaction processing.

NFR-17: All transactions shall be logged with timestamps and unique identifiers.

NFR-18: The system shall maintain backup logs for recovery.

2.7 Maintainability

NFR-19: The system shall follow modular architecture for future feature expansion.

NFR-20: The codebase shall follow clean documentation standards.

EDGE CASES:

1. Connectivity &amp; Synchronization Edge Case:

If the seller inputs the buyer's OTP while offline, the system must store the validation locally and sync with the server immediately when the connection is restored.

If the buyer attempts to pay in an area with a 2G connection, the system shall serve a lightweight, text-only payment link to ensure the transaction does not time out.

If the server is unreachable during an OTP attempt, show: 'Connection unstable. Please use the USSD fallback (*XXX#) to complete the release.

2. Transactional &amp; Security Edge Cases:

If the buyer enters an incorrect OTP three times, the system must lock the transaction for 30 minutes and notify the buyer via their registered email.

If the buyer refuses to provide the OTP after physical delivery, the seller shall trigger a 'Dispute: Refusal to Release' and the system must hold the funds for human mediation.

If a seller tries to create a payment link for a buyer already flagged for fraud, show: 'Transaction restricted: This buyer does not meet the minimum TrustScore™ requirement.

If the 48-hour inspection window expires without a buyer response, the system must automatically release the funds to the seller and mark the trade as 'Completed by Auto-Release'.&quot;

3. Verification &amp; Identity Edge Cases:

If a user’s Government ID photo is too blurry for the AI to read, show: 'ID verification failed. Please retake the photo in a well-lit area or use a different document.

If a user attempts to sign up with a phone number already linked to a banned account, show: 'Access Denied: This device/number is associated with suspicious activity.

If a seller’s TrustScore drops below 30, the system must limit their escrow capacity to $50 until they complete additional verification steps.

4. Financial &amp; Cross-Border Edge Cases:

If a buyer attempts to pay an amount exceeding their current KYC tier limit, show: 'Limit reached. Please upload your Government ID to process transactions above $X.

If a withdrawal to a mobile money wallet fails due to network downtime, the system must retry the disbursement automatically every 30 minutes for up to 4 hours.
